Do Elite NFL Offenses or Defenses Succeed More?

offense
defense
nfl
Author

Tysen Christie

Published

November 5, 2024

When watching an NFL football game, everyone loves to witness the highlight plays made by an offense or the hard hits that get laid by the defense. With this, there is sometimes that question lingering about which side of the ball matters more to their team. We all know that some NFL teams have, or have had some outstanding offenses or defenses throughout recent years and the past, but of these elite groups, which ones help their teams succeed the most? The first thing to look at when searching for elite offenses and defenses, is the points scored and points allowed by certain groups.

After visualizing this graphic, right off the bat you can point out some teams that have had great, and no so great offenses and defenses overtime. This could make you wonder, which of these teams have actually been successful? There have been many times where a team will have a top offense or defense in the league, but then lack tremendously in skill, depth, you name it on the other side of the ball. If you need an example, the 2022 Miami Dolphins. However, many things can happen during the regular season such as injuries or teams just having an off game, so their are many factors that could possibly go into poor performances by a certain side of the ball. Now I know everyone loves to see the flashy highlight plays made by star running backs and receivers, but people also love to watch defenses make highlight plays too, and sometimes those plays are some of the most impactful plays of the game or even season.

Thinking back to my main point of offenses and defenses making their teams “successful”, right away you can make some assumptions based off this graph. The Carolina Panthers, Tennessee Titans, and LA Rams clearly forced the least amount of turnovers last season, so did this affect their teams as a whole in any sort of way? Well, without doing any research, the Panthers and Titans were two bottom five teams in the entire NFL last year, but the Rams….. actually made the playoffs! One point that this could make us think about is that maybe offense is more important than defense? Well, maybe that is the case for the Rams at least considering they have two stellar wide receivers in Puka Nacua and Cooper Kupp and a second year running back in Kyren Williams. Don’t let me forget to mention that this Rams offense is also powered by veteran quarterback Matt Stafford as well. Aside from that, if you check out the defensive side of the ball when compared to the offensive side, you can make even more assumptions about certain teams.

It is no surprise that the LA Rams are here on this graphic with a positive point differential. Now it may not be quite as positive as some may have thought after seeing the Rams defensive turnovers, despite still making the playoffs, but there are many things that could play into that. For example, the Rams might not be getting very many turnovers on defense, but maybe they are also not turning the ball over much on offense either. Another thing to notice is that the Ravens had the highest point differential last year, and they were also the team with the most turnovers on the other graph. Most of the time these two things can go hand in hand with each other. However, back to the main point, the Chiefs are also in the same boat as the Rams. They didn’t force very many turnovers last year, but they were ranked very high in point differential and they also ended up winning the Super Bowl that same year. I think that there can still be many arguments made about which side of the ball helps their team succeed more, but I think these graphs do an outstanding job in showing that productive offenses in past years have done a great job at carrying the weight of their not so outstanding defenses.
